Ebola





Zaire ebolavirus

Ebola is a virus that causes Ebola Virus Disease (EVD), a deadly illness in people and non-human primates. It causes severe bleeding, organ failure and can lead to death.

SYMPTOMS : Requires a medical diagnosis. Initial symptoms include fever, headache, muscle pain and chills. Later, a person may experience internal bleeding resulting in vomiting or coughing blood. People may experience: Pain areas: in the abdomen, chest, joints, or muscles; Whole body: chills, dehydration, fatigue, fever, loss of appetite, malaise, or sweating; Gastro-intestinal: diarrhoea, nausea, vomiting, or vomiting blood; Also common: coughing up blood, eye redness, headache, mental confusion, red spots on skin, or sore throat.
TREATMENT : Treatment consists of fluids and supportive hospital care. Supportive care : Oxygen therapy, Supportive intensive care and IV fluids; Medications To reduce vomiting and diarrhea and to manage fever and pain; Blood transfusion.
PRECAUTIONS : EVD(Ebola Virus Disease) is most common in parts of sub-Saharan Africa. When living in or traveling to a region where Ebola virus is present, take preventive and protective measures like: Avoiding contact with blood and body fluids of an infected person or items belonging to them ; Not handling dead bodies or attending funerals of someone who died from EVD ; Avoiding contact with bats and nonhuman primates’ blood, fluids, or raw meat prepared from these animals (bushmeat) ; After returning from an area affected by Ebola, monitor your health for 21 days and seek medical care immediately if you develop symptoms of EVD. The two available Vaccines are protective against only the Zaire ebolavirus species of Ebola.
